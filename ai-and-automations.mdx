Custory AI is built into the product workflow instead of living as a separate detached destination.

## Where you can use AI

Custory AI appears in the places where teams already work:

- Inside journeys
- Inside persona work
- Inside automation setup
- In collaboration flows where AI is mentioned as a workspace participant
- In linked Slack and Discord workflows

## What AI helps with

Use the assistant to:

- Build or improve journey content
- Summarize evidence
- Review a journey for gaps or patterns
- Create or refine personas
- Draft automation messages and follow-up tasks
- Use connected tools such as GitHub, Linear, Jira, Notion, Slack, PostHog, and Stripe

## Build journeys with AI

Custory AI can help at several stages of journey creation.

Use it to:

- Draft the first journey structure
- Turn imported source material into stages and steps
- Refine unclear step names
- Suggest opportunities from existing evidence
- Review a journey for missing context or repeated friction

This is especially useful when you already have raw material from GitHub, Figma, Notion, or team discussion, but have not yet shaped it into a clean journey.

## Build personas with AI

Use AI in persona workflows to:

- Draft a first persona from workspace context
- Tighten loose or generic persona language
- Clarify goals, constraints, and problem patterns
- Refine a persona after the team learns more about a segment

AI is most useful here when you want a strong working draft quickly, not when you want a fully final research artifact without review.

## Build automations with AI

Use AI in automation workflows to:

- Draft clearer Slack or Discord updates
- Draft task titles and descriptions for GitHub, Jira, or Linear
- Write instructions for updating journeys from new signals
- Turn recurring team habits into a repeatable workflow

AI helps most when the automation needs context-aware text, not only static templates.

## AI as a workspace member

Custory AI has a consistent identity inside the workspace.

That matters because AI work becomes:

- Easier to invoke from the surrounding workflow
- Easier to track afterward
- Less likely to disappear into an unstructured side conversation

## Memory

Custory AI can use workspace-scoped memory to retrieve relevant context from earlier work.

When memory is enabled, the assistant can pull from:

- Prior AI and chat context
- Workspace-level knowledge
- Journey-related context where relevant

Turn memory off when you want the assistant to answer only from the immediate prompt and current surface.

## Connected integrations in AI chat

AI chat can work with connected integrations such as:

- Notion
- Figma
- Google Drive
- GitHub
- Linear
- Jira
- Slack
- PostHog
- Stripe

Use integration-aware prompts when the job depends on data that already lives in those systems.

## How to get the best results

Custory AI performs best when:

- The journey already has some real context
- Items are linked to clear evidence
- Owners, status, impact, and effort are reasonably current
- The prompt is tied to a concrete job, such as "review this journey for stale steps" or "draft a weekly Slack summary"

The assistant is strongest when it can work from structured workspace context instead of a blank page.

## Slack and Discord AI collaboration

Slack and Discord are not only notification targets. Teams can also use them as AI collaboration surfaces.

That lets you ask Custory AI for help directly in a thread, then keep the resulting work connected back to Custory.

## Auditability

Custory tracks AI activity so your team can see that work happened and inspect the outcome later.

That is especially important when AI is used for edits, follow-up generation, or multi-step operational work.
