Custory AI is built into the operating flow of the product.

It is most useful when it works from the same structured context your team is already maintaining inside journeys, items, personas, automations, and connected tools.

## Where AI appears

Custory AI can show up in workflows such as:

- Journey creation and refinement
- Persona creation and refinement
- Automation drafting
- Item-level collaboration
- Workspace-member collaboration
- Slack and Discord AI threads

## What AI helps with

Use AI to:

- Draft or improve journey structure
- Summarize evidence
- Reframe messy insight sets into clearer opportunities
- Draft better task handoffs
- Write clearer Slack or Discord updates
- Work from connected tools such as GitHub, Linear, Jira, Notion, PostHog, and Stripe

## What makes Custory AI different

Custory AI is strongest when it works from:

- Real journey structure
- Linked items
- Current statuses and owners
- Workspace memory
- Connected integrations

This gives it a better operational footing than a generic assistant that only sees a copied prompt.

## How to get better results

AI performs best when:

- The journey already has meaningful context
- Items are linked to evidence and to each other
- Ownership and status are reasonably current
- The prompt is tied to a concrete operational job

Good prompts:

- `Review this journey for stale assumptions`
- `Draft a Slack summary of the top 3 customer risks this week`
- `Turn these linked insights into two clear opportunities`

## Common mistakes

### Using AI before the workspace has usable structure

AI is much stronger when it is working from real context instead of a blank surface.

### Asking for a broad answer when you need a specific output

The assistant is more useful when the request has a clear job.

### Treating AI as the system of record

Custory should remain the durable home for the resulting decisions, items, links, and follow-up.
