Custory is built for shared work, which means the editor needs to stay understandable even when several people are changing the same journey at once.

## What you can see while the team is working

This page covers the real-time collaboration signals inside the journey editor.

## Why live collaboration matters in practice

When several people are working in the same journey, the team needs to see what is happening without stepping on each other.

Real-time collaboration reduces confusion during reviews, workshops, cleanup sessions, and live decision-making.

## Collaboration signals in the editor

Inside the editor, your team can see:

- presence avatars
- cursor movement
- active editing cues
- where someone is currently working

That makes it easier to understand who is changing what and where attention is focused right now.

<Info>
  Real-time visibility is most useful during live reviews and working sessions. It helps the team coordinate without turning the editor into a chat tool.
</Info>

## When to use it

Use real-time collaboration when:

- two or more people are reviewing the same journey
- a workshop is restructuring the map
- someone is cleaning up items while someone else reviews priorities
- the team wants to watch AI or teammate changes appear live

## Common mistakes

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Treating live editing like unlimited ownership">
    Real-time visibility helps coordination, but the team still needs clear decision ownership.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Making large edits without saying what is changing">
    The editor shows movement, not intent. A quick heads-up still helps when the change is significant.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Using live collaboration without narrowing the working surface">
    Filters and view changes make shared work much easier to follow in large journeys.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
