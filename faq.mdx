## What is a customer journey?

A customer journey is the path someone takes from first hearing about your product to becoming, or not becoming, a successful customer.

## Do I need to map every customer journey?

No. Start with one journey that matters now.

## What should we map first?

For most lean teams, start with onboarding, activation, trial to paid, support escalation, or renewal risk.

## What if I was invited to an existing workspace?

Join the existing workspace instead of creating a new one for the same team or product. Read [Invited to a workspace](/invited-to-a-workspace) for the clean path.

## How detailed should a journey be?

Detailed enough to support decisions. If every click becomes a step, the map becomes too heavy. If each stage is too broad, the team cannot act on it.

## Is Custory better than a whiteboard or Notion doc?

Usually yes once the team needs repeatable decisions from the same context. Custory keeps the journey, evidence, follow-up, AI work, and linked tasks connected instead of scattered.

## What is the difference between a journey and an item?

A journey is the overall flow. An item is a structured piece of context attached to that flow, such as an insight, opportunity, solution, metric, or touchpoint.

## Should I start from a blank journey?

Not always. Use blank when you know the journey well, [templates](/journey-templates) when you want a proven starting point, and [AI import](/ai-journey-imports) when the source already exists elsewhere.

## Can we use Custory without already knowing how journey mapping works?

Yes. Custory is useful for teams that are new to journey mapping, especially if you start with one focused journey and keep the first version simple.

## What does a good first journey look like?

Usually 4 to 7 stages, customer-language steps, a few high-signal items, and one small group of accountable editors.

## Can we use Custory AI without leaving the app?

Yes. Custory AI is built into [journeys](/journeys), [personas](/personas), and [automations](/automations).

## When should we use MCP?

Use [MCP](/mcp-server) when your team wants external AI clients such as Cursor or Claude to work directly with Custory workspace context.

## Can Slack and Discord do more than receive notifications?

Yes. They can receive automation updates and also support [AI collaboration](/slack-discord-ai-threads) and direct-message [notifications](/notifications) when personal accounts are linked.

## Can Custory create work in our delivery tools?

Yes. Custory can create and track follow-up work in tools such as [GitHub](/github-integration), Jira, and Linear. See [External tasks](/external-tasks).

## What kinds of automations can we build?

You can build scheduled and event-based [automations](/automations), including weekly summaries, PR follow-up, analytics checks, and task-creation workflows. See also [How automations work](/how-automations-work).

## How should we set up notifications?

Start with in-app [notifications](/notifications). Then add email or direct-message delivery only for the updates that truly deserve faster attention.

## How many people should edit the workspace at first?

Usually 2 to 5 editors. Start small and expand once the team has working habits.

## Is Custory only for product teams?

No. It is often most useful when product, support, success, and growth share the same customer map.

## Can we export or move work later?

Use linked tasks, integrations, and attachments intentionally so the reasoning stays portable. Confirm current export details in the product if export capability is part of your evaluation.

## How should we think about security?

Start with role-based [workspace](/workspace) access, scoped [integrations](/integrations), and revocable [MCP keys](/mcp-server). Then confirm any buyer-specific requirements during evaluation.

## Where should we look when something is not working?

Start with the page closest to the workflow that is failing. Use [Quickstart](/quickstart) for early setup, [Notifications](/notifications) for delivery issues, [Integrations](/integrations) for connected tools, and [How automations work](/how-automations-work) for repeated workflows.

## Does Custory work on mobile?

Custory is built primarily for operational work on desktop-sized surfaces. For any current mobile limitations or support expectations, verify in the product during evaluation.

## What if our team is very small?

That is a strong fit. Start with one journey, one small editor group, and only the integrations and automations that remove real friction.
