Custory notifications are designed to keep the right people aware of the right change at the right time.

For smaller teams, the challenge is not usually a lack of updates. It is deciding which updates deserve:

- In-app visibility
- Email
- Slack DM
- Discord DM

## The notification center

Custory includes an in-app notification center accessible from the notification bell.

The notification center is the default review surface for workspace activity.

## Inbox and archive

The notification center is split into:

- Inbox
- Archive

Use **Inbox** for active notifications that still may require attention.

Use **Archive** for notifications you want to keep out of the active stream without deleting the record from your personal history.

## What you can do in the notification center

From the notification center, you can:

- Open the related work directly
- Mark notifications as read
- Mark notifications as unread
- Mark all inbox items as read
- Archive items
- Move archived items back to inbox
- Load more older notifications

This is useful when Custory is part of a real operating rhythm instead of a tool you remember to check only occasionally.

## Notification categories

Custory groups notification preferences into three main categories:

- Mentions
- Item updates
- System

### Mentions

Notifications when someone mentions you in a comment.

### Item updates

Notifications for changes on items you are assigned to, including:

- Comments
- Assignment changes
- Status changes

### System

Notifications for operational events such as:

- Invitation updates
- Join request updates
- Automation failures
- Invalid automations
- Integration errors or expiry
- External task title or status changes

## Delivery channels

Custory supports these delivery channels:

- In-app
- Email
- Slack DM
- Discord DM

In-app is the default baseline. The other channels should be added intentionally for the updates that deserve faster attention.

## How to think about channel routing

Recommended pattern for lean teams:

- Keep most updates in-app
- Add email for durable catch-up
- Add Slack or Discord DM only for high-signal, time-sensitive updates

Examples:

- Mentions: in-app plus Slack or Discord DM
- Item updates: usually in-app only
- System: in-app plus email, and possibly Slack or Discord DM for high-responsibility roles

## Open related work directly

Notifications are most useful when they reduce navigation cost.

Custory notifications are designed to open the related work directly, so the person receiving the alert can move from signal to context quickly instead of hunting through the workspace.

## Best practices

### Use the notification center as your catch-up layer

If your team lives in Slack or Discord, it is still useful to keep the in-app bell as the durable summary of what changed.

### Keep direct-message channels high-signal

If Slack DM or Discord DM becomes noisy, the team will stop trusting it. Route only the updates that deserve interruption.

### Review system notifications seriously

Integration expiry, automation failures, and external task drift often signal operational decay before product teams notice it elsewhere.

## Common mistakes

### Sending everything everywhere

This usually creates fatigue rather than clarity.

### Using email for urgent operational updates only

Email is durable, but not always the fastest path. Use Slack or Discord DM when speed matters.

### Ignoring archive as a workflow tool

Archive is useful for clearing the active stream without losing the record of what happened.
