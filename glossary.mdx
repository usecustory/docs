## Core structure

### [Workspace](/workspace)

The shared home for one team, product, or product area in Custory.

Example: `Custory product team`

### [Journey](/journeys)

A map of one customer flow, such as onboarding, activation, or trial to paid.

Example: `Trial to paid`

### Stage

A major phase inside a journey.

Example: `Setup`

### Step

A specific customer moment inside a stage.

Example: `Customer connects Slack`

### [Item](/items)

A structured record attached to the journey that holds evidence, decisions, follow-up, or measurement.

Example: an insight about why users stall during setup

## Item types

### [Touchpoint](/touchpoints)

A customer-facing moment, surface, or interaction where the experience happens.

Example: `Invite email`

### [Insight](/insights)

A learning about the customer experience that explains what the team noticed or discovered.

Example: `Solo admins hesitate to invite teammates too early`

### [Opportunity](/opportunities)

A problem or leverage point the team may want to act on.

Example: `Reduce pressure at the teammate invite step`

### [Solution](/solutions)

A proposed or shipped response to an opportunity.

Example: `Add a skip-and-return-later path`

### [Metric](/metrics)

A number that tells you whether a problem matters or whether a response worked.

Example: `Invite completion rate within 3 days`

## Supporting context

### [Persona](/personas)

A reusable customer profile that helps the team interpret the journey from the right point of view.

Example: `Founder-led admin at a small SaaS team`

### [Signal](/signals)

Raw customer evidence from analytics, support, billing, feedback, or delivery work that can become structured journey context.

Example: a drop in setup completion after a product change

### [Automation](/automations)

A repeatable workflow in Custory that handles routine follow-up, updates, or orchestration work.

Example: a weekly journey summary posted to Slack

### [Integration](/integrations)

A connection between Custory and another tool your team already uses, such as Slack, GitHub, or PostHog.

Example: linking GitHub so shipped work can stay attached to the journey

### [AI workspace member](/ai-workspace-member)

AI working from the same workspace context as your team instead of from a one-off prompt.

Example: asking AI to summarize the latest evidence on an opportunity

## Working views and actions

### [Journey editor](/journey-editor)

The main workspace where you view the map, review items, compare priorities, and make updates.

### [Search and filters](/search-and-filters)

The controls that help you narrow the journey to the items, steps, or patterns you need to review.

### [External task](/external-tasks)

Linked delivery work in another system that stays attached to the customer context in Custory.

### [Version history](/version-history)

The record of saved journey changes that helps teams review edits and recover safely.

## How the chain works

### Evidence-to-action chain

The working flow from touchpoint to insight to opportunity to solution to metric.

Example:

- touchpoint: `Customer reaches the invite step`
- insight: `They are not ready to bring teammates in yet`
- opportunity: `Reduce pressure at the invite step`
- solution: `Add a skip path`
- metric: `Invite completion rate`

### Structured context

Customer evidence that has been attached to the journey, clarified with items, and made usable for decisions.

In plain English: not just "we heard something," but "we know where it belongs, what it means, and what to do next."
