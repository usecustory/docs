## What this is

This is a short overview of Custory's security and privacy posture for everyday evaluation conversations.

## Why it matters

Founders and lean teams still need clear security answers, even when they want to move quickly.

If the product will hold customer evidence, internal notes, and connected-tool context, the basics should be easy to understand.

## What Custory does

- Workspace access is role-based, so owners, editors, and viewers do not all have the same permissions.
- MCP access is workspace-scoped and revocable, which helps limit external AI access to one workspace at a time.
- Integrations and notification channels can be connected and revoked at the workspace level instead of being treated as permanent open pipes.

## Privacy expectations

- Do not put more customer data into the workspace than your team actually needs for the job.
- Use workspace separation when products, clients, or access boundaries should not share context.
- Revoke old MCP keys and expired integrations instead of leaving unclear access in place.

## What to confirm internally

If your buyer or security reviewer needs more detail, confirm the current status of topics such as:

- encryption in transit and at rest
- subprocessor coverage
- data retention
- compliance roadmap

This page is intentionally brief. It is meant to give an evaluator the first clear answer, not replace a deeper security review.
