Use this guide when you want the fastest path from empty workspace to a usable customer journey workflow.

## 1. Create a workspace

Create a workspace for one product, company, or product area.

Use one workspace when the same team needs shared journeys, personas, integrations, notifications, and automations. Create separate workspaces when you need clean separation between products, clients, or business units.

## 2. Invite the right people

Invite the people who already hold customer context:

- Product managers
- Support and customer success
- Design
- Engineering leads
- Founders or operators

Use roles to control who can edit. Owners and editors can maintain journeys and integrations. Viewers can follow context without changing the source of truth.

## 3. Create your first journey

Start with one path that matters now, such as onboarding, activation, trial-to-paid, support escalation, or renewal.

You can start in three ways:

- Create a blank journey
- Use a template
- Import a first draft with AI from connected sources such as GitHub, Figma, or Notion

## 4. Add stages and steps

Create stages for the major parts of the journey, then add steps for specific customer moments.

A good first journey usually has 4 to 7 stages and clear, concrete step names. Name steps after what the customer is trying to do, not after internal team labels.

## 5. Add items, not just structure

Once the map exists, add the context that makes it useful:

- Touchpoints
- Insights
- Opportunities
- Solutions
- Metrics

Open items to attach evidence, files, screenshots, links, comments, and owners. That is where Custory starts becoming operational instead of decorative.

## 6. Pick the right view for the job

Use:

- **Journey view** to tell the story
- **Grid view** to scan and review grouped work
- **Table view** to sort, filter, and clean up larger maps
- **Impact / effort matrix** to compare what to act on next

## 7. Turn on AI where it helps

Use the AI assistant when you need help drafting, restructuring, or reviewing work. It can:

- Draft or improve journey content
- Help create and refine personas
- Summarize evidence
- Suggest follow-up actions
- Use connected tools such as GitHub, Notion, Linear, Jira, Slack, PostHog, and Stripe

Leave memory on when you want AI to use workspace context from earlier work. Turn it off when you want a narrower answer.

## 8. Connect your core tools

Start with the tools your team already checks every day:

- Slack or Discord for team communication
- Linear, Jira, or GitHub for execution
- Notion or Google Drive for research
- PostHog or Stripe for product and revenue signals

Keep integrations focused. Connect only the tools that add signal to the journeys you actively review.

## 9. Set up one notification rule

Open **Settings** and configure notification delivery for mentions, item updates, and system events.

Use in-app notifications by default. Add email, Slack DM, or Discord DM for the updates that need faster attention.

## 10. Add one automation

Start with one small workflow, not a full automation program.

Good first automations:

- A weekly journey summary to Slack or Discord
- A daily focus check for high-priority items
- A GitHub PR merged workflow that updates the journey and posts a follow-up summary
