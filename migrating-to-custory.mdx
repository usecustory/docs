Use this guide when your customer journey already exists somewhere else and you want to move it into Custory without rebuilding it from scratch.

## What this is

Migrating to Custory means taking the customer context you already have and turning it into a working journey inside Custory.

That source material might live in:

- a CSV or spreadsheet
- a Notion page
- a workshop doc
- a whiteboard export
- a website or help center
- a GitHub repo
- a Figma file
- pasted notes from interviews, support, or onboarding calls

## Why it matters

Most teams do not start with nothing. They start with scattered material.

The goal is not to preserve every old artifact exactly as it is. The goal is to keep the useful customer context, place it on the journey, and give the team one system it can actually maintain.

For a lean team, that means:

- less manual rebuilding
- faster time to first useful journey
- a cleaner path from old notes to real decisions

## When to use it

Use this page when:

- the team already mapped the journey in a doc, spreadsheet, or board
- research notes already explain the customer flow
- onboarding or activation material lives across several tools
- you want AI to draft the structure before the team refines it

## How it works

The migration flow is usually straightforward:

1. choose the narrowest source that best explains one journey
2. bring that material into Custory through AI import or AI chat
3. ask Custory to draft the journey structure
4. clean up the stages and steps
5. attach the first real items so the draft becomes operational

Good sources are focused. A tight CSV, one strong Notion page, or one clean onboarding doc usually produces a better result than dumping everything in at once.

## What to import

Bring the source that already tells the customer story most clearly.

Examples:

- a CSV of onboarding steps, friction notes, or tagged feedback
- a Notion page with a workshop draft
- a public docs page that explains setup
- a repo or Figma file when the real flow is easiest to infer from the product itself
- pasted notes from support calls or customer interviews when the detail is not stored anywhere else

If you are using AI chat, give the model clear framing. Do not just paste content and hope it guesses the right scope.

Better prompt:

`Create a trial-to-paid journey for first-time workspace owners. Use this CSV and these notes to draft stages, steps, and the first likely friction points.`

## Example

Say your current onboarding journey lives in a spreadsheet with columns like:

- stage
- customer step
- friction
- evidence
- owner

You can bring that CSV into Custory, ask AI to draft the journey, then review the output:

1. keep the stages that match the real flow
2. rewrite vague step names in customer language
3. turn repeated friction notes into [insights](/insights) and [opportunities](/opportunities)
4. add the first [metrics](/metrics) that will tell you whether the journey is improving

The result should not be "our spreadsheet, but prettier."

The result should be a journey your team can review, update, and use for follow-through.

## Common mistakes

- importing every source at once instead of choosing one focused source
- treating the AI draft as final without cleanup
- preserving internal labels that do not describe the customer moment
- moving structure over but leaving the evidence behind

## What good looks like

A good migration gives you:

- one focused journey instead of a pile of imported material
- customer-language stages and steps
- a few strong linked items
- a map the team can review this week
