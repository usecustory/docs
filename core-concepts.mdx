Custory works best when your team shares the same model of how customer context is stored and moved forward.

## Workspace

A [workspace](/workspace) is the shared home for one team or product area.

It holds:

- Members and roles
- Journeys
- Personas
- Integrations
- Notification preferences
- Automations
- AI and MCP access scoped to that workspace

## Journey

A [journey](/journeys) is the main map of a customer flow.

It captures the experience in sequence, from one stage to the next, and gives your team a place to connect evidence, decisions, and follow-up work.

## Stage

A stage is a major phase in the journey.

Examples include discovery, signup, onboarding, activation, support, renewal, or expansion.

## Step

A step is a specific customer moment inside a stage.

Steps are where teams usually attach the most useful working context.

## Item

An item is a structured piece of context attached to the journey.

Custory supports five main item groups:

- [Touchpoints](/touchpoints)
- [Insights](/insights)
- [Opportunities](/opportunities)
- [Solutions](/solutions)
- [Metrics](/metrics)

Items can also carry properties such as status, priority, impact, effort, owners, comments, links, files, and history.

## Persona

A [persona](/personas) is a reusable customer profile for the workspace.

You can link personas to one or more journeys so the team can keep the right customer lens attached to the work.

## Integration

An integration connects Custory to another system.

That can mean:

- Sending updates to Slack or Discord
- Creating work in GitHub, Jira, or Linear
- Pulling context from Notion, Google Drive, Figma, or Miro
- Using PostHog and Stripe inside automations
- Exposing Custory to external AI tools through MCP

## Automation

An automation is a workflow that runs on a schedule or in response to an event.

It can fetch signals, update journeys, create tasks, and send messages across connected tools.

## AI assistant

Custory AI is a workspace participant, not just a detached prompt box.

You can use it inside journeys, personas, and automations. It can work with connected integrations, retrieve workspace memory, and post auditable results back into the product.

## MCP server

The MCP server lets external AI clients connect to Custory with workspace-scoped API keys.

That gives tools such as Cursor or Claude a direct way to read and update workspace context without broad account-level access.

## Key item types

### Touchpoint

A [touchpoint](/touchpoints) is a customer-facing moment in the journey, such as a page, email, support exchange, or in-app step.

### Insight

An [insight](/insights) is something your team learned about the customer experience.

### Opportunity

An [opportunity](/opportunities) is a problem or leverage point worth acting on.

### Solution

A [solution](/solutions) is a proposed or shipped response to an opportunity.

### Metric

A [metric](/metrics) is the number your team watches to understand whether the experience is improving or getting worse.

## Supporting concepts

### Version history

[Version history](/version-history) is the timeline of saved journey states that helps your team review, compare, and restore prior versions.

### Notifications

[Notifications](/notifications) deliver workspace and automation updates into the channels where your team already works.

## The working model

<Info>
  A workspace contains journeys, personas, integrations, automations, and members. Journeys hold stages and steps. Steps and stages hold items. AI, notifications, and integrations help the team act on what the journey reveals.
</Info>
