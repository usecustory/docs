Use this page when something in Custory is not behaving the way your team expects and you want the fastest path back to a usable workflow.

This guide focuses on common issues that are already reflected in the current product model and docs.

## Start with the layer that is actually failing

Most problems fall into one of five buckets:

1. workspace setup
2. journey structure
3. notifications or identity linking
4. integrations or automation setup
5. AI or context scope

If you identify the failing layer first, the fix usually becomes much simpler.

## I created the wrong workspace or duplicated one

This usually happens when someone was invited to an existing workspace but creates a new one instead.

What to do:

1. check whether the team already has the correct [workspace](/workspace)
2. use the existing invitation or ask an owner to resend it
3. move ongoing work into the intended workspace instead of maintaining two sources of truth

See [Invited to a workspace](/invited-to-a-workspace).

## The journey feels too broad to maintain

If the map becomes hard to review, the scope is usually too large.

Signs:

- too many stages for one decision
- step names are internal team labels instead of customer moments
- the team cannot tell what to review this week

What to do:

1. narrow the journey to one meaningful flow
2. rewrite steps from the customer's point of view
3. keep only the evidence that changes a decision

See [Quickstart](/quickstart) and [Your first journey (step by step)](/build-your-first-journey).

## Slack or Discord direct messages are not arriving

This is usually a two-layer setup issue.

Both conditions must be true:

1. the workspace Slack or Discord [integration](/integrations) is connected
2. the individual user has linked their own identity for DM delivery

If one layer is missing, DM delivery will not work reliably.

See [Notifications](/notifications).

## An automation is noisy, inactive, or not doing the right job

Common causes:

- the journey data is still messy
- the wrong integration is connected
- the trigger is too broad
- the AI instructions are unclear

What to do:

1. clean up the underlying [items](/items), ownership, and statuses
2. confirm the needed integration is connected
3. narrow the scope or filter conditions
4. start from a proven [automation template](/automation-templates) instead of rebuilding from zero

See [Automations](/automations), [How automations work](/how-automations-work), and [Build automations with AI chat](/build-automations-with-ai).

## AI cannot edit an automation the way I expect

Automation chat works against a saved automation record.

If the automation has not been saved yet, save it first, then reopen the AI workflow.

See [Build automations with AI chat](/build-automations-with-ai).

## AI or search is returning the wrong context

This often means the workspace or journey scope is too broad, or the underlying items are not specific enough.

What to do:

1. confirm you are in the correct workspace and journey
2. tighten vague step names and item wording
3. link evidence to the right customer moment
4. use [search and filters](/search-and-filters) before reviewing a large map

See [How Custory works](/how-custory-works), [Items](/items), and [Workspace memory](/workspace-memory).

## The team is not trusting the journey

That is usually a workflow problem, not a UI problem.

The common causes are:

- the map is outdated
- evidence lives outside the linked items
- shipped work is not connected back to the journey
- too many people edit without clear ownership

What to do:

1. run a short weekly review
2. reconnect the evidence-to-action chain
3. keep the editor group small
4. link shipped work back to the relevant opportunity or solution

See [Weekly workflows](/weekly-workflows) and [From mapping to shipping](/from-mapping-to-shipping).

## When to ask for help

If the problem is not explained by the workflow above, gather:

- the workspace or journey involved
- the step where the issue appears
- the connected integration or channel involved
- the exact behavior you expected versus what happened

That makes support or internal debugging much faster than a generic "it doesn't work."
