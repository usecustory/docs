Personas in Custory are workspace-level operating assets.

They exist to help your team make better decisions inside real journey work, not to become a separate documentation exercise.

## Why personas exist in Custory

Personas help the team answer:

- Who is this journey for?
- Which role is struggling here?
- Is this problem about the buyer, the admin, or the end user?
- Does this solution help the right customer?

Those questions matter most when the product serves several distinct roles, which is common in B2B and founder-led software teams.

## Workspace-level personas

Personas live at the workspace level so they can be reused across journeys.

That avoids a common failure mode where each team or each map creates its own slightly different version of the same customer profile.

## Journey linking

Personas become most valuable when linked to the journeys where they actually shape interpretation.

That is what turns them from static reference material into working context.

See [Persona linking](/persona-linking).

## Persona detail editing

Custory supports detailed persona editing across practical fields such as:

- Bio
- Role
- Role in journey
- Primary job-to-be-done
- Buying influence
- Budget or income
- Location
- Goals
- Frustrations
- Motivations
- Tech savviness
- Devices
- Tools and apps

See [Persona detail editing](/persona-detail-editing).

## AI support for personas

Custory AI can help with persona work by:

- Drafting a new persona
- Tightening generic language
- Expanding goals, frustrations, and motivations
- Suggesting which journeys a persona should be linked to

This is especially useful when your team has real customer understanding but has not yet shaped it into a reusable profile.

## When to create a new persona

Create a separate persona when a different role, workflow, or decision pattern changes how the journey should be interpreted.

Do not create a new persona for every minor variation.

## Good persona hygiene

For smaller teams:

- Keep personas specific enough to guide decisions
- Link them only where they matter
- Refine them over time as your product understanding improves

The right persona is the one that changes how the team frames the work.
