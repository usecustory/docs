Personas give your team a shared view of who the journey is for.

In Custory, personas are first-class workspace records, not isolated notes.

## What a persona should contain

Keep personas practical. Focus on the information that improves decisions:

- Name
- Role or situation
- Goals
- Friction or constraints
- What success looks like
- Linked journeys

## Workspace-level personas

Personas live at the workspace level so you can reuse them across multiple journeys.

That makes them easier to maintain and avoids duplicating the same customer profile in several places.

## Journey linking

Link personas directly to the journeys where they matter.

This helps your team answer:

- Who is this journey for?
- Which customer type is affected by this problem?
- Does this solution help the intended segment?

## AI support for personas

You can use Custory AI from the personas area to:

- Draft a new persona
- Tighten loose descriptions
- Clarify goals or pain points
- Refine persona language from existing workspace context

That makes personas easier to keep current as your product and customer understanding change.

## When to create a new persona

Create a new persona when a distinct role, workflow, or decision pattern changes how the journey should be interpreted.

Do not create separate personas for minor variations that do not lead to different product or support decisions.
