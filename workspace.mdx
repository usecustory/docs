A workspace is the shared operating layer for one team or product area in Custory.

## Members and roles

Use workspace roles to control who can edit and who can observe.

In practice:

- **Owners** manage the workspace, billing, and team structure
- **Editors** maintain journeys and operational context
- **Viewers** can follow the work without changing it

## Invitations and join flows

Custory supports:

- Direct invitations
- Invitation acceptance and decline
- Domain-based workspace discovery
- Join requests when someone needs access to an existing workspace

These flows reduce the setup friction that usually slows down collaborative tools early.

## Workspace settings

Workspace settings are where you manage:

- Name and slug
- Member roles
- Invitations and pending access
- Notification-related linking where relevant
- Integrations
- Billing and plan changes

## Billing

Workspace billing is managed at the workspace level.

The product supports:

- Plan selection
- Seat-aware billing
- Usage-based pricing controls where enabled
- Subscription management

Billing matters operationally because some capabilities, such as additional editors or usage-heavy workflows, may depend on plan configuration.

## Workspace-scoped AI and MCP access

AI memory and MCP access are scoped to the workspace, not to a global account context.

That means:

- AI can retrieve workspace-relevant context
- MCP keys can be created for a specific workspace
- Access can be revoked without broad account changes

## Good workspace habits

The healthiest workspaces usually have:

- A clear owner
- A regular review rhythm
- A small set of maintained journeys
- Integrations that match active workflows
- Notification rules that route only high-signal updates
