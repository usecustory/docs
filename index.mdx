Custory is an operating system for customer journeys. It helps your team turn scattered signals into structured context, then use that context to improve activation, retention, and advocacy over time.

## The problem Custory solves

Most teams do not struggle because they lack customer input.

They struggle because the input is split across too many places:

- support threads
- analytics dashboards
- billing events
- onboarding calls
- docs and research notes
- tickets and pull requests

The result is familiar. Everyone knows something important about the customer experience, but nobody has one shared map of what is happening, why it matters, and what the team should improve next.

## Why this matters for a product team

When the customer story is fragmented, product decisions get slower and weaker.

Teams start debating symptoms instead of seeing the journey clearly:

- activation drops, but nobody agrees where momentum is lost
- support sees repeated friction, but product cannot place it in the flow
- engineering ships fixes, but the original customer reason gets lost in delivery

That is where customer-journey thinking helps. It gives the team one shared model of the experience, so evidence, prioritization, and follow-through stay connected.

## How Custory works

Custory gives you a simple structure for turning that messy context into something operational:

**Workspace -> Journey -> Stage -> Step -> Item**

In practice, that means:

- your `Workspace` holds the shared operating context
- a `Journey` maps one important customer flow
- `Stages` and `Steps` describe the path from the customer's point of view
- `Items` capture touchpoints, insights, opportunities, solutions, and metrics

This is what makes Custory more than a static map. It is where the team can see the journey, attach proof, decide what to change, and keep learning after the change ships.

## What this looks like in practice

Imagine a small SaaS team trying to improve trial-to-paid conversion.

They already have useful signals:

- support chats about confusing setup
- product analytics showing drop-off after the first integration
- notes from onboarding calls
- GitHub issues for fixes already discussed internally

Custory helps them turn that into one working system:

1. map the `trial to paid` journey
2. place the friction on the right stage and step
3. turn the evidence into linked insights, opportunities, and solutions
4. choose what to improve first
5. track whether activation or conversion actually improves

That is the core promise: not more documentation, but faster clarity and better decisions from the context your team already has.

## Why lean teams use it

Custory is designed for teams that need wins quickly.

That usually means:

- founders
- product leads
- customer success leads
- early operators
- small cross-functional product teams

For those teams, the goal is not to model the whole business on day one. The goal is to get one journey useful enough that it improves a real product decision this week.
