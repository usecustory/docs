Use this page when you want the shortest clear answer to three questions: what Custory is, why it is worth your team's time, and what to do first.

Custory helps your team map the customer journey, attach real evidence to the moments that matter, and turn that context into better product decisions.

## What Custory is

Custory is a collaborative system for customer journeys.

You use it to:

- map a customer flow in one shared place
- keep evidence tied to the exact step where it matters
- turn scattered notes into clear insights, opportunities, solutions, and metrics
- connect the map to delivery, communication, and AI workflows

The core model is simple:

**Workspace -> Journey -> Stage -> Step -> Item**

That structure matters because it gives product, support, success, and founders the same map of the customer experience instead of leaving the story split across Slack, docs, tickets, and analytics.

## Why teams use Custory

Most teams already have customer context. They just do not have it connected.

Custory helps when your team needs:

- faster decisions
- less guessing
- fewer scattered notes
- clearer customer evidence
- better prioritization
- shared context that survives beyond one meeting

A whiteboard can align a team once. Custory is for when the journey needs to stay alive as the product changes, more evidence comes in, and follow-up work moves into other tools.

## When to use it

Custory is usually a strong fit when:

- onboarding or activation feels unclear
- trial-to-paid decisions keep getting debated
- support, product, and growth see different versions of the customer story
- important evidence lives across several tools
- you want to connect customer context to delivery and follow-up

Good first journeys are usually:

- new user onboarding
- activation
- trial to paid
- support escalation
- renewal risk
