Use this page when you want the short, practical answer to "What does Custory actually do for our team?"

Custory gives your team one shared system for understanding the customer journey, attaching evidence to the right moments, and deciding what to improve next.

That matters because most teams already have customer context. It is just scattered across calls, support threads, analytics, docs, tickets, and people's heads. Custory brings that context into one map so product, support, success, and growth can work from the same picture.

## The job Custory does

Custory helps you:

- map an important customer journey
- connect real evidence to the moments that matter
- turn observations into clear opportunities
- track proposed or shipped solutions
- measure whether the change actually helped

In other words, it helps your team move from "we think customers get stuck somewhere in onboarding" to "we know where they get stuck, why it matters, what we should change, and how we will measure it."

## Why teams use it instead of a whiteboard or doc

A static map is useful for alignment once.

Custory is useful when the team needs the journey to stay alive.

That usually means:

- the evidence changes every week
- multiple teams need the same context
- follow-up work lives in other tools
- you want AI and automations to work from real customer context, not isolated prompts

The difference is not just prettier mapping. The difference is that the journey becomes a working system instead of a stale artifact.

## What it looks like in practice

A lean SaaS team might use Custory like this:

1. Map the `trial to paid` journey.
2. Add steps like `Customer reads pricing page`, `Customer connects Slack`, and `Customer invites teammate`.
3. Attach touchpoints, insights, opportunities, solutions, and metrics to the right steps.
4. Connect Slack, GitHub, or analytics so the map stays tied to the real work.
5. Run a weekly review to decide what to improve next.

By the end of that workflow, the team is not guessing from scattered notes. They have one place to see the customer story, the supporting evidence, and the next decision.

## Where Custory helps most

Custory is usually strongest when you need faster clarity in flows such as:

- onboarding and activation
- trial to paid
- support escalation
- churn or renewal risk
- multi-step setup or rollout

## What it is not

Custory is not just a diagramming tool, not just a research repository, and not just an automation surface.

It works best when you use it as the operating layer between customer evidence and product decisions.
