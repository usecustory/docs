Opportunities are the problems, improvement areas, or leverage points your team may want to act on.

They sit between learning and delivery:

- More actionable than an insight
- More problem-focused than a solution

## When a pattern becomes an opportunity

An opportunity answers:

- What is worth improving here?
- What customer problem should we solve?
- Where is there leverage if we act?

Good opportunities are still problem-led. They do not assume the solution too early.

An insight becomes an opportunity when your team is ready to frame the problem clearly enough to compare it, prioritize it, or assign follow-through.

## The fields that help you compare it

Opportunities support:

- Description
- Status
- Owner
- Impact
- Effort
- Priority
- Confidence

### Impact

Impact uses a 1 to 5 scale.

Use it to estimate the upside if the opportunity is addressed well.

### Effort

Effort uses a 1 to 5 scale.

Use it to estimate how hard it may be to address, considering product, engineering, design, support, or operational change.

### Priority

Priority is the team's current ranking:

- High
- Medium
- Low

Priority is most useful after some comparison, not immediately on capture.

### Confidence

Use confidence when the problem framing is still uncertain, even if the customer signal feels real.

## How opportunity status changes the conversation

Opportunities use these statuses:

- New
- Framed
- Prioritized
- Parked
- Closed

Recommended interpretation:

- `New`: captured, but still rough
- `Framed`: clear enough to discuss or compare
- `Prioritized`: actively selected for attention
- `Parked`: intentionally not active right now
- `Closed`: resolved, no longer relevant, or intentionally retired

## How opportunities stay connected to the rest of the journey

Opportunities can link:

- Back to [Insights](/insights)
- Forward to [Solutions](/solutions)
- Forward to [Metrics](/metrics)

This makes them the central bridge between customer learning and action.

## Good opportunity examples

- `Reduce confusion before first integration`
- `Shorten the time between signup and first teammate invite`
- `Clarify billing recovery after failed payment`

Weak opportunity examples:

- `Build setup wizard`
- `Add button`
- `Rewrite docs`

Those are solutions, not opportunities.

## How to leverage opportunities inside Custory

### Prioritize with context, not ticket volume

Impact, effort, confidence, and linked insights help teams compare opportunities with more discipline.

### Assign owners only when follow-through matters

For small teams, owner fields work best when they represent real responsibility, not generic visibility.

### Link multiple solutions to one opportunity when needed

One problem can have several responses:

- A product change
- A docs change
- A service workaround
- A short-term experiment

Custory supports that without collapsing the problem framing.

## Framing mistakes to avoid

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Turning every idea into an opportunity">
    If the item already assumes the answer, write it as a solution instead. Keep the opportunity focused on the problem or leverage point, not the chosen response.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Using opportunity titles that are too broad">
    `Improve onboarding` is usually too large. Frame the actual leverage point so the team can compare it, prioritize it, and act on it clearly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Prioritizing without linked evidence">
    High priority means more when the reasoning is visible. Link the opportunity back to the insight or touchpoint that created it so the team can see why it matters.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to create one

1. Open the step or linked insight that reveals the problem, then click **Add item** and choose **Opportunity**.
2. Title it as the problem or leverage point, not the answer, then set `Impact`, `Effort`, `Priority`, and `Confidence`.
3. Save it and link the supporting [insights](/insights), candidate [solutions](/solutions), and any early [metrics](/metrics) that will help you compare it.
